There are three phases in the surgical process: preoperative, in traoperative, and postoperative. These three phases are referred to collectively by the word perioperative,
which is defined as the time surrounding and during surgery.②
The preoperative phase starts when the client is apprised of the need for surgery and ends when anesthesia is induced.
The int raoperative phase begins with the induction of anest hesia and finishes when the surgical procedure is completed. The postoperative phase covers the time from when the surgery ends and con tinues until the healing process is completed.
In the preoperative phase, the client undergoes a series of diagnostic tests and physical examinations to ascertain his or her present state of health. The nurse obtains a nursing history to determine the client's nursing care needs and to identify actual or potential negative health problems. Once the nursing history hasbeen obtained and negative health behaviors havebeeniden tified, the nurse can plan to intervene. All clients must sign an informed consen t before any surgical process. If the client is under 18 years of age or incapable of signingt he form, the closest relative does so.
